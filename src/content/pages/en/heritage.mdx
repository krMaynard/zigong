The "old" in Zigong is not a vague nostalgia. It is several quite different timescales that happen to be stacked on the same red sandstone.

The deepest layer is roughly 160 million years old. The [Dashanpu bonebed](/en/dinosaurs/) preserves an entire Middle-Jurassic fauna in the Shaximiao Formation, and the museum is built directly over the dig.

Above it lies human time. For nearly two thousand years people here have drawn brine from far underground to boil into salt; by 1835 the [Shenhai well](/en/salt/) reached past a thousand metres — the first borehole on Earth to do so, using only bamboo, wood, and muscle.

Neither history has sunk quietly into the past; both are still visited and retold, and — in the case of salt — still worked. Begin with those two threads: salt and dinosaurs.
